Personal Profile

My name is Marco Kleimans and as of today, I am . I am currently living in Houston, Texas, with my amazing girlfriend who is finishing her Master's degree at Rice University. I was born in Moscow, Russia, but spent most of my childhood in Buenos Aires, Argentina, where I grew up. I then moved to Los Angeles, California, where I graduated from the University of Southern California with a Bachelor's and a Master's degree in Environmental Engineering. After graduation – and as a result of life decisions and opportunities – I decided to pivot away from Environmental Engineering and started working as a Web Developer at the Information Sciences Institute.

As Jostein Gaarder claims - my favorite writer and philosopher from Norway - embracing uncertainty and accepting one's shortcomings is a critical first step towards self-improvement and realization. His book "The Solitaire Mystery" has been a hallmark for me, as it narrates the story of a father and son who relentlessly search for their missing wife and mother, while strange encounters with mythical creatures along the way inspire a though-provoking exploration of the meaning of life. This is, without doubt, a metaphor of meaning, of reaching the unthinkable, and being able to enjoy the journey while welcoming the unexpected.

This story is my banner: I always enthusiastic about facing new challenges that force me to step outside my comfort zone, but I am also pragmatic and always looking forward to learning and developing new skills. I consider myself methodical and organized, persevering and energetic, and both flexible and always well-disposed. I'm passionate about new technologies, and optimistic about the future. Having a multicultural background allows me to analyze complex situations from different perspectives to find the most fitting solution for each scenario. I enjoy learning, sharing, and helping others accomplish their goals, approaching all tasks with open-mindedness and excitement.

Work Experience

Web Developer I

Information Sciences Institute (ISI)

July 2021 - Present

Remote (Marina del Rey, California)

HTML, CSS, PHP, JavaScript, SQL, WordPress, Beaver Builder, GitHub, Jira, Affinity Designer, Microsoft Office

The Information Sciences Institute is a world-class leader in cutting-edge computing technologies: AI, Quantum Computing, Space Research, Microchips, Networking and Cybersecurity, Social Media and Health, and many others.

I started my job at ISI as a student worker in July 2021. As a result of my commitment, malleability, and adroitness, I was later offered a full-time position as a Web Developer I. Working as a web developer at ISI meant that I had to adapt to a very fast-paced, constantly evolving environment and had to learn new technologies and concepts from scratch. Environmental Engineering is not computer-heavy, and programming is not part of the core curriculum. However, I accepted this challenge and, step-by-step, learned the tools needed to become a proficient entry level developer capable of completing and excelling at my tasks in the institute.

Not only did I learn programming languages like HTML, CSS, PHP, and Javascript, but I also learnt how to use tools like WordPress, BeaverBuilder, WP Engine, Jira, Trello, GitHub, the terminal, SharePoint, and the Affinity Suite, among others. I also had to collaborate with staff from different departments, organize and share work-progress during meetings, and provide training for some of our platforms. Last but not least, I was involved in promoting and catering DEI efforts, as well as accessibility compliance for ISI's website.

Among the most relevant projects I completed, I helped migrate more than 300 websites from Drupal - the old Content Management System (CMS) used at ISI - to WordPress, which involved moving more than 1000 webpages from an on-premise server to the website hosting service WP Engine. This included redesigning the websites and adapting the content and media to the new layout using WordPress, custom-built themes, and Beaver Builder. I was also largely in charge of maintaining ISI's documentation website (Action), which involved creating documentation, designing the layout, and providing the most reasonable information. For more detailed information and other projects, please see the Projects section below.

Water Treatment Researcher

McCurry Lab

March 2019 - May 2021

Los Angeles, California

Analytical Chemistry, Organic Chemistry, Liquid-Liquid Extraction, Solutions/Dilutions, Water and Wastewater Treatment

After my first year and a half at USC, I wanted to explore practical implementations of environmental engineering. After researching available possibilities, I ended up working for Dr. Daniel McCurry in the McCurry Lab at USC.

Throughout my years of experience in the lab, I was in charge of making solutions and dilutions of water samples to test for the formation of byproducts using a mass spectrometer. Some of the techniques used involved liquid-liquid extraction, UV radiation, and pH buffering, among others.

Self-discipline and consistency are a must. Some days, I would spend more than 5 hours at once working in our projects. Being a researcher teaches one to be patient and helps one understand that reaching goals means repetition, practice, and perseverance.

After several years of research, I was able to help Lily - current post-doc at Stanford - publish a paper on the fate nitrogenous disinfection byproducts (nitromethane) during ozonation in water reuse processes. The paper is linked below:

Shi, J.L., Plata, S.L., Kleimans, M., Childress, A., & McCurry, D.L. (2021). Formation and Fate of Nitromethane in Ozone-Based Water Reuse Processes. Environmental science & technology.

Product Management Intern

URUS City

June 2019 - July 2019

Moscow, Russia

Product Management, Product and Market Research, Product Knowledge, Slide Creation, Presentation

Moscow is one of the most technologically advanced, breathtakingly modern cities in the world. Politics aside, the Russian tech sector is a powerhouse, and companies are constantly seeking to be at the forefront of development.

URUS City is a well-established monitoring company that specializes in air pollutants. During my internship at URUS, I was told that the company was trying to create a water monitoring device and was asked to do research on this topic.

In a period of 8 weeks, I conducted market research and investigated papers and existing products, as well as water quality requirements, monitoring markers, water chemistry and physics, and electronics. I was also communicating updates during bi-weekly meetings with the team. Once the internship was over, I had to provide a final presentation with the ideas and recommendations in front of the entire company, which I did successfully.

During my brief but intense internship I developed strong communication and technical skills, some project management tools, and practice in giving presentations.
